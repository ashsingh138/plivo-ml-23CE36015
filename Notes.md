My best configuration leverages a custom Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) tokenizer (vocab=768), which drastically compresses the raw byte sequences—especially for Devanagari characters that otherwise hijack the context window. Tying the embedding and LM head weights (`tie_weights = True`) freed up roughly 150,000 parameters, which were reinvested into widening the embedding dimension (`n_embd=192`) while remaining safely under the 2,000,000 parameter cap. I adopted Llama-style architectural tweaks by swapping LayerNorm for RMSNorm and dropping all linear biases to maximize parameter efficiency and CPU throughput. To ensure the script finished within the 120-minute window, I forced PyTorch to utilize all CPU threads and dropped gradient accumulation. Training stability was secured by swapping to AdamW (weight decay 0.1) and a Cosine Decay schedule with a 200-step linear warmup, aggressively pushing the learning rate to 2e-3 to compensate for the smaller batch size. These combined optimizations compress the data logically, expand the context dynamically, and stabilize convergence effectively.
